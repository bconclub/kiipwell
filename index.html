01 — Organisation

Who We Are

Kiipwell Solutions is a supported housing provider operating in the North East of England. We work exclusively with young people aged 16 to 21, including unaccompanied asylum-seeking children (UASC), care leavers, and young people at risk of homelessness or social isolation.

Our team combines professional backgrounds in housing management, safeguarding, and statutory youth support with direct lived experience of the asylum process — enabling a uniquely informed approach.

02 — Services

What We Offer

A structured programme of accommodation and specialist support services, commissioned through local authority and health frameworks across the North East.

Safe Semi-Independent Accommodation
Well-maintained, registered properties with 24/7 staff on-call and regular safety inspections.
Dedicated Key Worker Support

05 — Methodology

Our Approach to Supported Living

01

Person-Centred Planning

Every support plan is built around the individual — their history, goals, language needs, and pace. Reviews held at regular intervals with active participation from the young person.

02

Trauma-Informed Practice

All staff trained in trauma-informed approaches. Our environment and interactions are designed to build safety and trust, not to retraumatise — recognising the weight of adverse experience.

03

Culturally Responsive Delivery

We employ staff with lived experience of the asylum journey. Support is delivered with genuine understanding of language barriers, cultural norms, and religious observance.

Statement of Purpose

"To provide safe, registered accommodation where young people can develop the skills and confidence needed for independent living."

Committed to outcomes-focused delivery, collaborative working with commissioning bodies, and continuous improvement through feedback and inspection readiness.

06 — Safeguarding

Our Safeguarding Commitment

Safeguarding is a statutory requirement and a central organisational priority. Our policies and staff competencies are maintained to the standard required by commissioning local authorities.

Comprehensive Safeguarding Policies
Up-to-date policies covering child protection, adults at risk, missing persons, county lines, and online safety. Reviewed annually.
Enhanced DBS and Vetted Workforce
All staff hold enhanced DBS clearance. Safer recruitment procedures applied including references, employment history verification, and interview assessment.
Clear Referral and Reporting Procedures
Designated Safeguarding Lead in post. All staff trained in recognising and reporting concerns. Clear referral pathways to MASH and children's services.
Partnership with Statutory Authorities
Active engagement with local authority children's services, leaving care teams, and HNSS. Regular liaison with placing authority social workers and LAC reviews.
