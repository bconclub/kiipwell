01 — Organisation

Who We Are

An independent registered provider delivering structured, specialist accommodation and wrap-around support for young people across the North East.

Kiipwell Solutions is a supported housing provider operating in the North East of England. We work exclusively with young people aged 16 to 21, including unaccompanied asylum-seeking children (UASC), care leavers, and young people at risk of homelessness or social isolation.

Our team combines professional backgrounds in housing management, safeguarding, and statutory youth support with direct lived experience of the asylum process — enabling a uniquely informed approach to complex needs.

05 — Methodology

Our Approach to Supported Living

01

Person-Centred Planning

Every support plan is built around the individual — their history, goals, language needs, and pace. No template-driven care. Reviews are held at regular intervals with the young person's active participation.

02

Trauma-Informed Practice

All staff are trained in trauma-informed approaches. We recognise that many young people carry significant adverse experiences. Our environment and interactions are designed to build safety and trust, not to retraumatise.

03

Culturally Responsive Delivery

We employ staff with lived experience of the asylum journey and cultural contexts relevant to our client group. Support is delivered with genuine understanding of language barriers, cultural norms, and religious observance.

Statement of Purpose

"To provide safe, registered accommodation where young people can develop the skills and confidence needed for independent living."

We are committed to outcomes-focused delivery, collaborative working with commissioning bodies, and continuous improvement through feedback and inspection readiness.

06 — Safeguarding

Our Safeguarding Commitment

Safeguarding is a statutory requirement and a central organisational priority. Our policies, procedures, and staff competencies are maintained to the standard required by commissioning local authorities.

Comprehensive Safeguarding Policies
Up-to-date policies covering child protection, adults at risk, missing persons, county lines, and online safety. Reviewed annually and following any significant incident.
Enhanced DBS and Vetted Workforce
All staff and volunteers hold enhanced DBS clearance. Safer recruitment procedures applied across the organisation including references, employment history verification, and interview assessment.
Clear Referral and Reporting Procedures
Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) in post. All staff trained in recognising and reporting concerns. Referral pathways to MASH, local authority children's services, and police clearly established.
Partnership with Statutory Authorities
Active engagement with local authority children's services, virtual school heads, leaving care teams, and HNSS. Regular liaison with placing authority social workers and participation in LAC reviews.
